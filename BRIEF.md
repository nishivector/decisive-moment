ONE VERB: SNAP
GAME IN 10 WORDS: A street photographer snaps fleeting moments before subjects look away
WHAT THE PLAYER MASTERS: Composing a shot by waiting for the precise frame — subject, light angle, and background element must align simultaneously in the viewfinder
THE WOW MOMENT: At the end of level 3, your best photo is printed and displayed in a gallery wall with a score and crowd reaction. The crowd's applause is proportional to your composition quality — a perfect shot triggers a visible gasp and a single tear from a painted portrait on the wall behind the gallery-goers.
WHY RICARDO: Thumper's rhythm violence reframed — the alignment of three elements creates a beat you feel before you see, and pressing at the wrong moment ruins the frame with a shutter-click that sounds like a snare hit on the wrong beat. Inside's atmosphere through restraint — the world is alive and ignoring you, and the tension is in not disturbing it.
EMOTIONAL ARC:
  start: One subject in warm afternoon light. They glance away and back. The window is obvious. You snap. Perfect.
  groove: Two subjects interacting, a pigeon about to land in the background, a shadow crossing from the left. You're conducting time.
  climax: A rainstorm. Subjects scatter. One woman pauses under an awning, lightning about to strike behind her, a child turning to look. The window is 0.4 seconds. You've been building to this shot.
WHAT MAKES IT HARD: Each element (subject pose, background event, light quality) cycles at a different, non-synced rhythm. You cannot force alignment — only read and wait. Acting too early on two aligned elements misses the third.
SKILL CEILING: A 1x player snaps when two things align and hopes for the third. A 10x player memorises each element's cycle length, calculates the next triple-alignment in advance, and enters the frame pre-composed — scoring bonus "anticipation" points for pressing exactly on beat.
VISUAL: #E8D5B0 (warm golden-hour tan background), #2C5F4A (deep teal shadow), #F4A822 (amber light burst) — first-person viewfinder perspective, slight vignette on the frame edges, rule-of-thirds grid faintly visible. Reference: Monument Valley's geometric elegance meets a film photography zine.
AUDIO: 78 BPM, warm upright bass walking line with a solo trumpet motif that resolves only on a perfect shot — signature sound: a clean analogue shutter CLICK that echoes slightly, like a shot taken in a cathedral
NOT: No time-pressure panic mechanic where the subject runs away. No hidden object / spot-the-difference puzzle.
GAME NAME HINT: decisive-moment
